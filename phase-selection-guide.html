Sprunki Phase Guide

Choose a Phase experience around a creative goal and a trustworthy browser page—not a claimed “best” ranking. A clear source, suitable content, and a stable play session matter more than a numbered list.

1. Start With the Sound Goal

Different Phase pages can aim for different moods, arrangements, or fan-made variations. Start by naming what the player wants to make or hear, then choose a focused experience instead of opening a long list of unrelated pages.

  • Choose a mood or sound direction.
  • Keep the first session short and exploratory.
  • Save only the names that match the goal.

2. Do Not Treat a Number as a Quality Score

A Phase number is a name, not evidence that one experience is newer, safer, official, or better. Check the page itself: does it explain what it contains, who hosts it, and whether it behaves like a normal browser experience?

  • Prefer clear titles and direct pages.
  • Avoid fake download or update prompts.
  • Use the safety guide before installing anything.

3. Keep Notes That Help Creativity

Instead of ranking pages, note the sound style, whether the browser controls worked, and whether the content was appropriate for the intended player. This creates a useful personal catalogue without inventing popularity data.

  • Record the page title and source.
  • Note the sound style in a few words.
  • Leave pages with unexpected or unsuitable content.

4. Link Focused Pages Together

A good Phase guide should help a reader choose a safe next step. It should not push every game in a large directory or claim ownership of fan-made projects.
